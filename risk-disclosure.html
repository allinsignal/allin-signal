4. Market Risk

All investments carry risk. Market conditions can change rapidly and unpredictably. Specific risks include but are not limited to:

  • Equity risk: Stock prices can fall significantly, including to zero.
  • Volatility risk: Markets can experience sudden and extreme price swings.
  • Liquidity risk: Some securities may be difficult to buy or sell at desired prices.
  • Systemic risk: Broad market downturns can affect all securities simultaneously.
  • Concentration risk: Investing in a small number of stocks increases exposure to any single company's performance.
